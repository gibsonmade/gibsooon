# The Home Depot

Helping the Nation's Largest Home Improvement Retailer Sell Projects, Not Just Products

Status: flagship, draft ready for Gibson review.

## Draft Summary

Gibson helped lead UX design work at The Home Depot around project-based shopping patterns: digital tools that asked customers the right questions about projects like countertops, decks, cabinets, window treatments, carpet, and drywall, then helped them build more complete material lists instead of shopping item by item.

## The Challenge

HomeDepot.com has a massive catalog, but home improvement projects are not usually solved by buying one product. Customers often need a combination of materials, quantities, options, and services. For projects like installing countertops, building a backyard deck, updating cabinets, choosing custom window treatments, replacing doors, estimating carpet, or calculating drywall, customers can miss items, choose the wrong materials, or need to return to the store multiple times.

The challenge was to make a huge retail catalog behave more like a guided project system: ask the right questions, collect the right inputs, help customers make confident decisions, and generate a material list they could reference, add to cart, pick up locally, have delivered, or split across pickup and delivery.

## Gibson's Role

Gibson was hired by The Home Depot as a Senior Manager of UX Design from 2017 to 2021.

In that role, Gibson managed and led a team of product designers and UX researchers. He helped spearhead reusable, validated UX patterns for project input flows across HomeDepot.com, while partnering with developers, business owners, analysts, product teams, designers, and researchers.

Gibson was also responsible for supporting the team itself: coaching associates from junior to senior, senior to principal, mentoring designers and researchers, running workshops, and speaking internally about product design, organizational best practices, design systems, and A/B testing.

## The Work

Gibson helped the team create universal patterns for guided project experiences. Those patterns covered:

- The right number of questions to ask
- The types of questions customers could answer confidently
- Input patterns for measurements, materials, shapes, and options
- Page layout patterns
- Mobile considerations
- Research and validation standards
- Use of the Home Depot design system
- E-commerce and omnichannel best practices

The work helped teams create tools that could guide customers through project-specific decisions instead of making them assemble a complete material list manually from the catalog.

## The Outcome

Gibson helped The Home Depot contribute reusable, validated UX patterns to its digital ecosystem. The work supported a shift from product-by-product shopping toward project-based material selection.

For customers, the value was clarity: enter project details, select materials, understand the needed list, and move toward purchase, pickup, delivery, or a combination of both.

For the organization, the value was scale: stronger shared patterns for calculators, configurators, project flows, and cross-functional delivery across teams.
