# Mini Murals

Transforming a Grassroots Art Project Into a City-Wide Digital Experience

Status: flagship, draft ready for Gibson review.

## Draft Summary

Gibson helped Mini Murals turn a Houston public-art initiative into a brand, website, interactive map, and digital discovery experience.

## The Challenge

Mini Murals transformed gray traffic-control cabinets around Houston into public art. The project worked with the City of Houston, artists, districts, and community partners to bring creativity into everyday streets, intersections, and neighborhoods.

The physical work was distributed across the city: more than 250 murals by more than 40 artists. The digital challenge was making that network discoverable. People needed a way to find murals by neighborhood, artist, and theme, and the organization needed better tools to communicate, organize, market, and grow the project.

## Gibson's Role

Gibson joined Mini Murals around 2012 and helped through about 2016 as CTO.

He helped the CEO and COO execute the idea, bring organization to the operation, create the logo, build and maintain the website, create interactive mural maps, support email and marketing campaigns, and keep the project moving with practical tools like Trello and WordPress.

## The Work

Gibson helped create the digital and operational foundation around the public art program:

- Logo and brand support
- Website creation and WordPress updates
- Interactive digital maps
- Neighborhood, artist, and theme browsing
- Trello-based project organization
- Email campaigns
- Marketing material
- Custom campaign testing
- Digital storytelling around murals, artists, neighborhoods, and themes

## The Outcome

Mini Murals helped bring 250+ public artworks by 40+ artists to Houston.

The digital work made the murals easier to discover, whether someone was exploring an art district, passing through a neighborhood, sitting in traffic, or finding public art in areas that had limited professional public art before.

The project received the Keep Houston Beautiful award, was nominated by the Mayor's Office of Cultural Affairs, and Gibson won an Emerging Business Leader award connected to the project and his participation with the organization.

## Story Angle

This story is about bringing civic creativity online.

It should prove that Gibson can help an early-stage creative organization operationalize a strong idea, build the digital infrastructure, organize the work, and make a public experience more accessible.
